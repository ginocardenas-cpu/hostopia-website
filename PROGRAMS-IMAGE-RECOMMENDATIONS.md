# Programs Pages — Image Recommendations

## General Guidance

These pages are selling professional services (not software features), so the imagery should feel **human, collaborative, and business-focused** — not stock-photo generic. Avoid typical "smiling person with headset" or "handshake in a boardroom" cliches. Instead, lean toward imagery that suggests active partnership: real teams working together, data on screens, structured processes in action.

**Format**: JPG for photos, PNG for any graphics with transparency. Aim for 1600–2400px wide for hero images (Next.js `Image` will optimize).

**Style consistency**: All hero images should share a visual tone — warm lighting, professional environments, muted/desaturated color grade that complements the teal (#2CADB2) and cream (#f7f6f2) palette. Avoid overly saturated stock photos.

## Hero Images (One per Page)

### Channel as a Service
**File**: `public/programs/channel-as-a-service-hero.jpg`

**What to show**: A focused, professional sales environment — think a modern office or command-center style workspace with people actively engaged on calls or screens showing dashboards/CRM data. The imagery should suggest *organized outreach at scale*, not a chaotic call center.

**Ideal subjects**:
- Small team (2–4 people) at workstations with headsets and dual monitors showing dashboards
- A modern open-plan office with branded screens or dashboards visible
- Split composition: person on a call on one side, analytics dashboard on the other

**Avoid**: Generic call center rows, stock "thumbs up" poses, empty desks

**Where to source**:
- Unsplash: search "sales team office", "customer success workspace", "modern call center"
- Shutterstock/Adobe Stock: search "B2B sales team", "outbound sales office", "CRM dashboard team"

### Partner Marketing
**File**: `public/programs/partner-marketing-hero.jpg`

**What to show**: Collaborative marketing planning — whiteboard sessions, campaign planning, or creative review. Should feel like a marketing team working *with* someone (the partner), not just presenting to a camera.

**Ideal subjects**:

- A team reviewing data/charts together with laptops open

**Avoid**: Solo person at laptop, generic "brainstorming" stock photos, anything too startup-y/casual

**Where to source**:
- Unsplash: search "marketing team collaboration", "campaign planning whiteboard", "marketing strategy meeting"
- Shutterstock: search "B2B marketing team", "demand generation planning", "marketing collaboration"

### Project Management
**File**: `public/programs/project-management-hero.jpg`

**What to show**: Structured project delivery — Gantt charts, project timelines, organized handoffs. The imagery should convey *control, clarity, and methodology* — this is about reassuring partners that complex projects are in good hands.

**Ideal subjects**:
- A project timeline or Gantt chart on a large screen with a PM presenting or discussing with a colleague
- Someone reviewing a project plan on a tablet or laptop with timeline/milestone views visible
- A clean desk with a project plan document, laptop showing progress dashboards, and a coffee mug (signals a working professional, not a model)

**Avoid**: Construction site project management imagery, generic "teamwork" photos, overly staged "pointing at screen" poses

**Where to source**:
- Unsplash: search "project management dashboard", "project timeline screen", "project planning"
- Shutterstock: search "IT project management", "deployment planning", "migration project"

### Service Management
**File**: `public/programs/service-management-hero.jpg`

**What to show**: A trusted relationship — one-on-one or small group professional interaction that feels like a genuine business conversation, not a sales pitch. Should suggest *partnership and accountability*.

- A service manager presenting a QBR (quarterly business review) slide to a small group

**Avoid**: Generic "handshake" photos, stock boardroom scenes, anything that feels transactional

**Where to source**:

- Shutterstock: search "quarterly business review", "account management meeting", "client success manager"

### Support
**File**: `public/programs/support-hero.jpg`

**What to show**: Professional, multilingual customer support in action. Should feel *competent and calm* — not frantic. The vibe is "your customers are in good hands."

**Ideal subjects**:
- A support agent at a clean workstation with a headset, focused and professional — possibly with a second screen showing a ticketing system
- A diverse team of support agents in a modern, well-lit support center (not a cramped cubicle farm)
- A support dashboard showing live metrics (CSAT, resolution time, ticket volume) with an agent in the foreground

**Avoid**: Outdated call center imagery, cheesy "How can I help you?" poses, stock photos of people pointing at screens

**Where to source**:
- Unsplash: search "customer support professional", "technical support agent", "help desk modern"
- Shutterstock: search "white-label customer support", "multilingual support team", "24/7 support center"

## Alt Text Guidelines

Every image must have descriptive alt text for accessibility and SEO. Follow this pattern:

```
[Subject performing action] + [relevant context]
```

**Examples**:
- "Sales team consulting with SMB customers on behalf of a service provider partner"
- "Marketing team reviewing a demand generation campaign on a shared screen"
- "Project manager presenting a deployment timeline dashboard"
- "Service manager conducting a quarterly business review with a partner"
- "Technical support agents delivering multilingual customer support"

## Budget-Friendly Options

If sourcing professional photography isn't an option right now:

1. **Unsplash** (free) — Good quality, search terms above will surface usable results. May need to try several queries.
2. **Pexels** (free) — Similar to Unsplash, good for professional/business imagery.
3. **Shutterstock / Adobe Stock** (paid) — Higher quality, more specific results. Worth it for hero images.
4. **AI-generated imagery** — Tools like Midjourney or DALL-E can create custom professional scenes. Useful for getting exactly the composition you want, especially for abstract/conceptual shots. Use carefully — some AI imagery still looks uncanny in professional contexts.
5. **Custom photography** — Highest quality but highest cost. If you go this route, shoot all five hero images in one session for visual consistency.

**Recommendation**: For launch, use Unsplash or Pexels for hero images and invest in 1-2 custom visuals for the hub page and Support page (your highest-traffic program pages). Replace with custom photography later as budget allows.
